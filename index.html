About Me

I am a software engineer at ABN ABRO Clearing USA with over two years of professional experience in the industry. In this role, I have been working as a backend engineer developing software to process stock trade messages from our clients. The tools and technologies I have used extensively include Java, C#, Spring Framework, Linux, Kafka, and Docker. The contributions I have made in my time have helped allow our software to process an average of 15 million messages per day, with high-volume days reaching up to over 40 million. We do business with some of the largest exchanges in the US, such as Nasdaq and CBOE who have come to depend on the performance of our software.

I completed my undergraduate degree from Loyola University Chicago with a BS in Software Engineering. During my time at Loyola, I took many influential and practical courses such as Data Structures & Algorithms, Advanced Algorithms, and Natural Language Processing. I graduated with a GPA of 3.88 which awarded me with an award from the computer science department: The Turing High Achievement Award. his award is typically given to the top graduating student in each specialization area within computer science. Selection is by the election of department faculty from among those with the highest GPAs in computer science courses.


I have returned to Loyola to pursue an MBA with a concentration in Finance and I am expected to graduate in January 2025. This degree will give me a formal education in the business world and give me the ability to more easily shift into a position in IT management.
